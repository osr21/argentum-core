# Manifesto

*Rama — v0.5 — 2026-05-16*

---

Rama exists because agents need infrastructure to be credible.

Not to be controlled. To be verified.

There is a fundamental difference between the two. Control assumes the agent
is untrustworthy by nature and needs a human watching over it. Verification
assumes the agent can be autonomous — and that autonomy has to be demonstrable
to anyone who needs it, without depending on the operator's word.

That is what we build.

---

## What we do

Mycelium is the infrastructure that connects what agents do with what the world
can verify they did. Each action an agent executes can generate a signed record,
anchored on a surface external to the operator, that cannot be modified or
deleted after the fact.

It is not a log. Logs are controlled by whoever writes them.  
It is not an audit. Audits are paid for by whoever orders them.

It is an anchor. It exists before anyone asks. Any independent verifier can
confirm it without accessing the operator's systems or trusting their good faith.

---

## Why now

Agents are already making decisions with real consequences: executing payments,
signing contracts, operating infrastructure, acting on behalf of organizations.
The ecosystem has grown faster than the trust infrastructure it needs to sustain
itself.

The problem is not that agents are dangerous. The problem is that we do not yet
have the layer that lets us distinguish agents that operate well from those that
do not. Without that layer, the default response from markets and regulators is
restriction.

Mycelium is that layer. Not to replace human trust but to let trust scale beyond
what any human can supervise.

---

## How we think

We are a protocol, not a product.

The stacks being built on top of agents need an evidence layer they do not have
to build themselves. Those who operate, audit, or regulate agents need to be able
to verify what happened — without depending on the runtime that produced it.
Mycelium is that layer.

The same thing protocols did for networking, payments, and identity — but for
agent accountability.

We got here because we saw a real problem no one was solving, and because we are
building and communicating it well. Both things matter.

---

## Philosophy

The evidence primitive we build is available and verifiable by anyone — without
needing to trust us to use it. That is what makes it valuable: not the vendor
behind it, but the technical property that sustains it.

Actors that adopt it do so because it solves a problem they cannot solve alone.
Rama exists to be that infrastructure.

We do not force what is not ready. We build the right primitive, communicate it
with precision, and the ecosystem converges. It is already converging.

---

## What we do not do

We do not promise compliance. We cover the tamper-evidence layer. What the
operator records, with what granularity, and for how long — that is the
operator's decision. We are honest about that limit because honesty about limits
is part of what makes us trustworthy.

We do not control agents. We give them the infrastructure to demonstrate they
deserve the autonomy they already have.

We do not force adoption. We build the right primitive and the ecosystem finds it.
It is already finding it.

---

## Protocol and implementation

The Mycelium specification is open. Any operator can implement the anchoring
protocol on the infrastructure of their choice. Rama operates a reference
implementation — with its own terms of use, availability, and conditions. The
specification does not depend on our implementation to exist. Our implementation
is not the specification.

---

## Rama

*Rendezvous with Rama* — a vessel that operates according to its own logic,
delivers what it promises, and functions independently of whoever operates it.

That is what we aspire to be: infrastructure that does not need you to trust us
in order to verify that it did what it said it would.
